## Context

What is the problem, and what forces are at play? Describe the technical, business, and team constraints that make a decision necessary. State facts, not the chosen solution. A reader should be able to feel the tension that demands a choice.

## Decision

The decision, stated in active voice: "We will …". Be specific enough that someone could implement it without re-deriving it.

## Alternatives considered

Each realistic alternative, with why it was not chosen. Recording rejected options is the point of an ADR — it prevents re-litigating settled questions.

## Consequences

What becomes easier and what becomes harder as a result. Include the costs and risks accepted, and any follow-on work or constraints this imposes on future decisions.
